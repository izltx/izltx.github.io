2. Executive Summary

On March 30, 2026, the SOC received automated alerts triggered by Splunk monitoring Event ID 4625 (Failed Logon Attempts). Investigation revealed a high-volume RDP brute-force attack originating from external IP 10.10.50.100 targeting the Administrator and SVC_Backup accounts. Following 1,600+ failed attempts, the actor successfully compromised the SVC_Backup credential. Once inside the perimeter, the attacker immediately attempted to establish persistence by creating a rogue administrative account named hacker_account, triggering Event ID 4720. The SOC team successfully contained the threat by terminating the session, isolating the affected machine, and blocking the ingress IP.

5. Containment & Eradication

Phase Actions Taken by SOC
Containment 1. Terminated active RDP session for compromised account SVC_Backup.
2. Isolated CORP-DC1 temporarily to prevent lateral movement.
3. Implemented temporary block rule for 10.10.50.100 on edge firewall.
Eradication 1. Deleted unauthorized user hacker_account and purged associated registry keys/profiles.
2. Terminated all suspicious rogue processes spawned by the attacker.
3. Conducted full EDR scan on the Domain Controller (No malware persistence found).

6. Recommendations & Remediation

  • MFA Implementation: Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all external-facing RDP endpoints and critical Service Accounts.
  • Network Perimeter Block: Permanently block the malicious external IP 10.10.50.100 at the Edge Firewall and update Threat Intel feeds.
  • Credential Reset & Audit: Immediately force a password reset for the compromised SVC_Backup account and conduct a comprehensive audit of its assigned permissions across the domain.
